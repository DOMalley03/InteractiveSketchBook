Interface System Self Reflection

Navigation Model

20pts

I focused on implementing clear navigation more than any other part of the project. I wanted to have icons on the bottom that signify moving between sketches and the homepage. Similarly, I wanted to organize sections based on the type of webpage rather than the weekley format that was originally there.

Hierarchy & Mobile Logic

18pts

The main thing that I focused on for this aspect was having the buttons easier to tap on for screen users. This involved making the link buttons larger and a little more seperated for users using one hand. Although I think I did well, I should have found a way for the hover function to work on the phone version.

Hiearchy & Compression

14pts

I wanted to change the hiearchy of information to be more clear to the user. This involved changing the text sizes, organization of information, and adding bounding boxes to sections. Adding the hover function to the tabs on desktop also helped show the user what section they were about to click on before they selected it. On the mobile version the buttons are even more seperated and centered on the screen so that it was easier for both right handed and left handed people to access the pages.

Theoreticl Integration

15pts

I took a lot of inspiration from the Heuristics of Neilson for the creation of the webpages. I wanted to add visibility of system status by addding clear webpage names, and hover features. I also kept the pages consistant between Micro-project and Sketches which was also done on mobile (but in a centered format). I also implemented the minimalist design in the landing page. Adding the buttons to the bottom of the pages also gave the users more freedom which is talked about in flexibility of use. For Shneiderman's 8 Golden Rules, I tried to strive for consistancy between the desktop and mobile versions. Informative feedback was seen in the navigation model and hover functions. Short term memory load was also seen in navigation between multiple sketches and the landing page. This also helped with the easy reversal of actions in case the user pressed on the wrong section. for Norman's design principals I wanted to add signifiers to make the affordances easier to spot for the user. This included added the icons from Font Awesome as well as the bounding boxes to the sections in the landing page. The hovor function on the landing page also helped with giving the user feedback for their choices.

Cross-Page Consistency

10pts

The three pages use the same minimalist design as well as the same layout for buttons. This is mirrored on the mobile version of the site despite the information being centered on this format. The desktop and the mobile version share the same bounding boxes for the sections although the sizes change between the two formats. I think the sites stayed as clear as possible while looking like they belong in the same system.

Risk & Structural Ambition

4pts

One risk was changing the format of the webpage from a weekly format to a section format. I was not sure this was going to be clear, but after implementing it, I thought the landing page seemed a lot more structured. Similarly, I think adding the dropdown menus to each section rather than a dropdown menu to the top was different from other sites. Other sites usually use the top dropdown or the running head with the other sections. I wish that I took more risks but I also felt very unfamiliar with how figma worked. Getting to know how to use the site was half of the battle for me.

Iterative Development

10pts

I wanted to keep all the previous iterations in figma as references to come back to. I did not know that I could have the different pages which was something I learned from the last critique. I titled each frame with the week number as well as added a screenshot of what the page actually looked like. This helped me plan out what I wanted to change and also look at what designs worked and what didnt. Specifically with the sections, being able to see the previous iterations made me realize the organizaiton of the info was not working.

Participation

5pts

I feel that I was able to get a lot of good feedback from my breakout groups when showing my figma project. John and Liam were especially helpful when trying to organize my information and make the site mobile friendly. I also tried my hardest to look at the systems of my peers through the lens of a new user to give them feedback as an outsider. Looking at their systems also really helped me with plan what changes I wanted to make on future iterations of the project.
